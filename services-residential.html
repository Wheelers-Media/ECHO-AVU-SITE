Should I Use A TV Or Projector?
Both have pros and cons. What is the size of the room? How much ambient light is there? A dedicated theatre room that can be darkened favors a projector, whereas a living room with ambient light leans more towards a TV.
How Many Speakers Do I Need?
Generally, the larger the room, the more speakers you can accommodate. However, quality takes precedence over quantity. Another factor is source compatibility (Netflix supports 5.1/Atmos, but apps via game consoles might not).
What Is Dolby Atmos?
Dolby Atmos adds a height channel to create a 3D audio experience. Instead of hearing a helicopter go around you, you hear it go directly over your head via in-ceiling or upfiring speakers.

Distributed Audio

A distributed audio system allows you to listen to sound from a central source throughout the house. This typically consists of installing speakers on the walls, ceiling, or underneath flooring; then routing signals through an amplifier to control volume levels in each room.

After decades of distributed audio installations, we can help you plan your budget.

Multi Channel Vs Multi Zone Audio
A multi-zone system gives you independent control over groups of speakers. For example, setting the volume in your living room at a different level than in your backyard requires a multi-zone system.

How Many Access Points Do I Need?
Consider the size, layout, and construction (concrete floors? metal studs?). Also, the distance from the router to the furthest point. All these factors determine the number of WiFi access points needed.
Does My ISP Matter?
As long as you are getting fast enough upload/download speeds, the choice is yours. We are familiar with all modems/routers and can work with any ISP.
Are There Too Many Competing Networks?
Interference is possible. We utilize hardware and software to generate a 'heat map' of the WiFi coverage in your home to ensure the least amount of interference.
